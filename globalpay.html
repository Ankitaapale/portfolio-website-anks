Sector : Finance / Fintech
Role : Designing the entire product from scratch from research to conception, visualization and testing
Timelines : 3 months
Tools used : Figma, Optimalworkshop, UsabilityHub, Google forms

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Online payments and transactions have increased by 65% over the last 2 years, with a 45% increase in remote jobs around the globe, making it difficult for individuals and businesses to make or receive payments, convert or move money. High transaction fees charged on every single transaction make it hard for users to maintain economical and convenient control over their finances.

POSSIBLE SOLUTIONS

  • Allowing users to move their money internationally with a low and transparent fee structure
  • A way for users to hold, convert, receive a salary or pension, and pay bills in multiple currencies
  • Its important to have a crypto-friendly option that allows users to make transactions using different crypto currencies
  • To avoid hacks, attacks, and fraud, an app must adhere to the strictest security standards
  • Information on the app should be clear, easy, and simple to understand
  • Providing information and customer support in multiple languages

AFFINITY MAPS

I started by conducting user surveys and user interviews, which helped me gain in-depth knowledge and understanding about the users, analyze information, and find unique data. It was a wonderful experience to understand the users needs and goals. It was interesting to see how different users can have similar goals but different needs.

KEY FINDINGS

  • Most users have more than one e-wallet account for their different needs. They would love to have a single e-wallet
  • It's important for users to have a good exchange rate and low transaction fees while making cross-country transfers
  • Many users have cryptocurrencies, but they don't have an easy way to store and convert crypto to cash, as crypto e-wallets are very complicated and vulnerable to hacks
  • Notification of every transaction is crucial for users
  • Users are afraid of fraudulent activities
  • It's important for users to have all the information and charges clearly and transparently laid out
  • Users are not happy with the rewards and referral plans e-wallets have to offer
  • Users feel safe with well-established e-wallets. They are more convinced to try it when someone recommends it

MEET OUR USER PERSONAS

Based on my findings, I built user personas, user journey maps, and mental models, which enable me to develop empathy for our users and relate to them better. User journey maps and mental models allowed me to have a user-centered focus.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

The sitemap for the app was developed and modified by conducting card sorting, which proved to be extremely helpful in getting the structure of the app. Also, it became really easy to visualize how individual screens would relate to each other.

LOW [&] MID FIDELITY PROTOTYPING

Low and mid fidelity prototypes were created to quickly test the design by conducting usability testing, which will help catch the errors and refine the overall product.

USABILITY TEST RESULTS

For this study of the Global Pay app, usability tests were conducted with six different participants. The participants are located all around the world. I learned a lot while conducting these usability tests and found some valuable key insights regarding different features, structures, and elements of the app. Getting feedback throughout the design process helped me a lot to work in the right direction.

FINAL PROTOTYPE

The final prototype of the app was created by understanding and implementing the principles of material design which helped make the app look more professional. Also followed the principles of accessibility to improve the design of the global pay app to cater to a wider audience.

How it works, charges for the transactions and its limitations, but the most important thing for me was to understand the users. It was a wonderful experience to understand the users. Conducting user surveys and interviews helped gain in-depth knowledge and understanding about the users, analyze information, and find unique data.
